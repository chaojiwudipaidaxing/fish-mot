Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, "A drift-aware and deployment-oriented evaluation framework for fish multi-object tracking in digital aquaculture," for publication in Computers and Electronics in Agriculture.

This submission is intentionally engineering-oriented. We do not make winner-takes-all claims over strong baselines. Instead, we contribute an investigator-developed framework that improves evidence reliability and supports tracking-layer default selection under a shared detector source for aquaculture vision systems.

The core novelty is a coupled protocol:
1) audit-ready reproducibility controls with drift-robustness monitoring (run_config locking, manifest hashing, data-drift gates),
2) controlled degradation protocol for robustness diagnosis,
3) quantile stratified failure analysis,
4) count stability metrics,
5) runtime/resource profiling with tracking-layer deployment guideline generation, where Scope B runtime evidence is used for end-to-end system-cost support rather than detector-coupled accuracy claims.

The frozen release bundle includes editable text tables (CSV/TEX), figures, and reproducibility scripts with traceable metadata (git_commit and file hashes). This aligns with CEA's practical emphasis on verifiable agricultural computing systems.

We believe the manuscript can be useful to researchers and practitioners auditing tracking-layer choices in fish tracking/counting pipelines under real resource constraints.
